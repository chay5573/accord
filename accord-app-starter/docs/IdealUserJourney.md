# Accord Ideal User Journey

The natural Accord journey is **Capture → Prepare → Manage**. It follows the agent's work instead of forcing transaction administration before useful work can begin.

## Capture

1. A client conversation begins.
2. Accord captures it with confirmed consent, or recovers the information from a recap, transcript, note, document, or attributable activity signal.
3. Accord works quietly: identifying people, property, intent, terms, stage, and likely destination without requiring a transaction form.
4. When evidence is incomplete, Accord preserves uncertainty and asks only what it cannot safely determine.

## Prepare

5. Accord prepares the appropriate editable documents from approved forms and attributable facts.
6. The agent reviews Accord's work and answers only unresolved blockers.
7. The agent approves the editable draft package.
8. The agent clicks **Send for Signature** through the preferred provider review flow.

The target from conversation end through signature send is fewer than three minutes, no more than five manual decisions, 15–20 clicks, almost no typing, and at least 90% of information inferred.

## Manage

9. Signed documents return to Accord for agent final review.
10. Deal Desk manages the active transaction, deadlines, documents, activity, exceptions, and next actions.
11. The client receives guided, transaction-aware education through Accord Guide using approved client-visible facts and documents.
12. The transaction closes with a complete, auditable record.
13. If separately authorized, redacted, and human-approved, Transaction Memory improves future mappings, workflow suggestions, and office patterns.

## Recovery expectations

The journey must survive forgotten recordings, incomplete facts, interrupted reviews, provider delays, and later evidence. The agent should resume from the last meaningful decision without re-entering information or reconstructing the workflow manually.

## Journey governance

- Capture is about preserving value with minimal interruption.
- Prepare is about AI work, evidence review, and a small number of human decisions.
- Manage begins after paperwork is prepared or explicitly saved.
- Settings configure policy; they are never part of the routine journey.
- Human approval remains mandatory for legal documents, signature sending, and external sharing.
