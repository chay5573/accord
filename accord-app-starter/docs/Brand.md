# Accord Brand

## Positioning

Accord is the composed, intelligent deal desk for premium real estate professionals. The brand should communicate command, discretion, accuracy, and human judgment.

## Brand promise

**From conversation to confident transaction.**

Supporting language should emphasize reviewed intelligence: Accord organizes facts, surfaces uncertainty, and prepares work for professional approval.

## Personality

- Assured, never loud.
- Precise, never clinical.
- Premium, never ornamental.
- Helpful, never overconfident.
- Protective of the agent-client relationship.

## Voice and language

Use short, direct sentences and familiar real estate vocabulary. State what happened, what Accord inferred, and what the agent must decide. Prefer “suggested,” “recommended,” and “ready for review” over “completed” when human approval is outstanding.

Never imply that Accord provides legal advice, guarantees compliance, understands unstated intent, or can approve a contract for the user.

## Visual direction

The starter establishes the core palette:

- Deep navy (`#071B33`) for trust, navigation, and primary action.
- Platinum and silver (`#D8DEE8`, `#AEB7C6`) for structure and supporting detail.
- Warm cream (`#F7F6F2`) for a quieter premium workspace.
- White for active work surfaces.
- Restrained green, amber, and red for state—not decoration.

Use generous spacing, strong hierarchy, modest radii, and subtle depth. Dense transaction detail should remain scan-friendly. Confidence must not rely on color alone.

## Product terminology

- **Deal Desk:** portfolio-level transaction workspace.
- **Transaction:** the canonical deal container.
- **Office Profile:** agent, team, or brokerage defaults and playbook.
- **Accord Cloud:** Accord-managed canonical file storage.
- **Contract Library:** versioned forms and mappings managed in Settings.
- **Recommended Package:** forms suggested from deal facts and policy.
- **Agent approval:** explicit human confirmation of material terms.

## Trust language

Clearly label AI-produced content, confidence, source evidence, recording state, approval state, and synchronization state. Avoid celebratory copy for legally consequential actions; confirmation should be calm and specific.
