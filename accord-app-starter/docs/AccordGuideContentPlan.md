# Accord Guide Content Plan

## Purpose

Accord Guide content supports transaction-aware explanations; it is not a generic FAQ or video library. Accord produces its own governed content rather than embedding random public videos. Recommendations consider transaction stage, approved forms and documents, upcoming deadlines, client role, jurisdiction, prior questions, and signature status.

## Production standard

- Target length: 60–180 seconds.
- Tone: premium, calm, plain-English, and confidence-building.
- Accessible captions and transcript are required before approval.
- Script structure: what it means; why it matters; what the client should watch for; when to ask the agent.
- Educational only. Scripts do not provide legal, tax, lending, inspection, or negotiation advice.

## Content governance

Every article, explanation, and video records topic, transaction stage, form/document and section, jurisdiction/state, buyer/seller role, status (`draft`, `approved`, or `retired`), reviewer, reviewed date, compliance notes, version, replacement content when retired, source citation, and visibility. Only approved, current, jurisdiction-matched content is client eligible.

Multilingual readiness includes language, locale, translated title, translated transcript, translated captions, and translation review status. No translation is part of the current mock.

## Initial categories and videos

- Offer basics: “Reading Your Offer Roadmap.”
- Earnest money: “Earnest Money in 90 Seconds.”
- Due diligence: “What Due Diligence Means.”
- Seller disclosures: “Seller Disclosures Explained.”
- Inspection objection: “Understanding the Inspection Objection Stage.”
- Financing/appraisal: “Financing and Appraisal Basics.”
- Title/escrow: “What Title and Escrow Do.”
- Settlement/closing: “Understanding Settlement.”
- Signing documents: “What Happens After Signing?”
- What happens next: stage-specific next-step explainers.

## Recommendations and measurement

Unlock rules use current stage, forms in the package, deadlines, client role, question history, and signature packet status. Future privacy-safe analytics may track videos viewed, questions asked, sections clicked, unanswered questions, agent escalations, and aggregate confusion topics. Analytics must not become an unrestricted transcript or sensitive-question store.
